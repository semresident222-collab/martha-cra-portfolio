CRA-Portfolio

Clinical Research Associate Portfolio | Martha Wuya Koroma
Three independent governance analyses of high-profile clinical trials. Each identifies a structural failure in trial monitoring or regulatory oversight, then proposes a concrete, guideline-anchored intervention.

What's Here

Door 1 — ADUHELM (Aducanumab)
Neurology / Regulatory. Analyses how independent evidence evaluation became optional during approval. Proposes an Independent Evidence Integrity Unit (IEIU) with DMC Governance Lock and Sponsor-Regulator Interaction Audit Trail.

Door 2 — KEYNOTE-991 (Pembrolizumab + Enzalutamide)
Oncology. Identifies cytotoxic chemotherapy assumptions applied to immune checkpoint biology. Proposes Adaptive Futility Boundary with immunotherapy-aware conditional power, plus Integrated Safety-Efficacy Monitoring with IPCW analysis and structured irAE rechallenge.

Door 3 — Adult ADHD Digital Biomarker Feasibility
CNS / Neurodevelopmental. Phase II site feasibility assessment for a digital biomarker-aided diagnostic protocol targeting adult West African women with unrecognised ADHD in Berlin. Three-tier biomarker battery: actigraphy, continuous performance testing, smartphone passive monitoring. GDPR Article 9 privacy-by-design architecture.

What This Is

Independent analytical exercises, not commissioned work. Each document:
Identifies a specific structural failure
Traces it to regulatory and methodological roots
Proposes a concrete intervention anchored in ICH guidelines (E6(R2), E9, E9(R1)), FDA/EMA guidance, and peer-reviewed literature
Names what is certain, what is structurally confident, and what is genuinely open

Doors 1 and 2 analyse concluded trials (Aduhelm approved 2021, KEYNOTE-991 discontinued 2023). 
Door 3 proposes a feasibility study that does not yet exist.

Who This Is For

Hiring managers evaluating CRA or clinical operations candidates. Researchers interested in trial governance architecture. Anyone who believes that reading the architecture, not just the results, is a trainable skill.
